About

I am an incoming M.E. student in Computational Science and Engineering at Harvard University. My research interests include robust representation learning, multimodal learning, and geometric machine learning. I am particularly interested in understanding how learned representations can remain reliable under distribution shift, and how multimodal signals and geometric structure can help models generalize across datasets and tasks. More broadly, I hope to develop mathematically principled learning methods that are reliable in embodied and scientific applications. Previously, I earned my bachelor's degree at the University of Michigan, where my research involved evaluating foundation-model representations, designing algorithms for infinite mathematical structures, and developing statistical methods for scientific data.

Research Experience

Laboratory of Geometry at Michigan (LoG(M))

Undergraduate Researcher · University of Michigan · Supervised by Dr. Grant T. Barkley

Ann Arbor, MI · Aug. 2025–Jul. 2026

Developed algorithms, data structures, and research software for computation with translation-invariant total orders, a class of infinite mathematical structures.

  • Developed finite representations of inversion sets, reducing weak-order comparison between translation-invariant total orders from an infinite problem to a finite computation.
  • Co-designed an efficient data structure and algorithm for comparing two translation-invariant total orders in the weak order.
  • Introduced graph representations of inversion sets and designed a graph-based algorithm for computing joins, or lowest upper bounds.
  • Implemented and open-sourced TITO_Explore, a Python package for TITO-related computations, and led the drafting and revision of the accompanying preprint.
